Staking XAND takes about five minutes. Choosing where to stake it is the part that actually decides your rewards, and it is the part most guides skip. Here is the whole process: what staking does, the exact steps, how to judge a pNode like an operator would, and the honest risks.

What staking XAND actually does

Xandeum is a decentralized storage network, blockchain-agnostic by design and live today on Solana. Storage providers (pNodes) earn STOINC, a share of wSOL fee pools that settle once per Yuga (about 2.8 days). Each node's share is set by its weight:

weight = storage × performance × stake × boost

Stake is one of the four multipliers. When you delegate XAND to a node, you raise its weight, and in return you receive a share of the node's rewards proportional to your stake, minus the operator's commission. Two things follow from the multiplication that most people miss:

  • Your rewards ride on the node's performance. The same stake on a 99% uptime node and an 85% uptime node earns very different amounts. Where you stake matters as much as how much.
  • A node with no stake earns nothing at all. Which is why operators (including us) compete for delegation, and why your stake has real bargaining power.

Before you start: three things you need

  • XAND tokens. XAND trades on Raydium (Solana's main DEX). Centralized exchange pages listing XAND are price trackers, not places to buy it.
  • A Solana wallet such as Phantom or Solflare, holding your XAND.
  • A little SOL in the same wallet for transaction fees.

How to stake, step by step

Pick your pNode first

This is the decision that matters, so do it before opening the portal. Use the live node table: sort by Staked, then compare Uptime, Health and Version. The next section tells you exactly what to look for.

Open the official staking portal

Go to xand-staking.xandeum.network (the only official portal; be suspicious of anything else) and connect your Solana wallet.

Select the node

Select or paste the pNode address you chose. Double-check it matches the node you researched, and note the commission shown.

Enter the amount and confirm

Enter how much XAND to stake and approve the transaction in your wallet. There is no officially published minimum.

Verify it landed

Within a short while your stake shows against the node in the dashboard's Staked column (tap the node for details). Save the node to My Nodes to watch its uptime and get a free alert if it ever goes offline.

What rewards to expect (the honest part)

Rewards arrive in wSOL each Yuga, roughly 130 times a year. How much depends on the node's share of network weight and on the size of the fee pool, and the pool follows real storage usage. Today, network storage is under 1% used, so pools, and therefore staking yields, are small. We published the full breakdown with live data and scenario math in How much does a pNode earn?, and the same logic applies to your delegated share.

Plain words: stake XAND because you believe storage usage on Xandeum will grow and you want weight in the system when it does, not because of today's yield. Anyone quoting you a juicy current APY is not being straight with you.

Risks, stated plainly

  • Token risk. XAND is a micro-cap token trading only on Raydium. Volatility dwarfs any staking yield; the token price move will dominate your outcome either way.
  • Pool risk. STOINC pools depend on paid usage arriving. It may take longer than anyone hopes.
  • Operator risk. A badly-run node earns you less. Mitigate it with the checklist above and by monitoring after you stake.
  • Smart contract risk. Staking happens through on-chain programs; bugs are always possible anywhere in crypto.
  • Terms can change. Unstaking rules and program parameters are defined by the protocol and shown in the portal; check them at the time you act.

FAQ

How do I stake XAND?

Get XAND on Raydium plus a little SOL for fees, open the official portal at xand-staking.xandeum.network, connect your Solana wallet, select a pNode, enter the amount and confirm. About five minutes end to end.

What does staking do?

It multiplies the node's reward weight (storage × performance × stake × boost) and entitles you to a proportional share of the node's STOINC rewards, minus the operator's commission.

How often do rewards arrive?

Once per Yuga, roughly every 2.8 days, in wSOL. The amount depends on the node's weight share and the pool size; pools are small today because storage usage is under 1%.

Is there a minimum stake?

No officially published minimum. You need a little SOL for transaction fees.

How do I choose the node?

Compare uptime, health, commission and version on the live dashboard. Net yield = the node's performance times your share after commission, so a reliable node at 10% usually beats a shaky one at 5%.

Can I unstake?

Yes, from the same portal. Rules and timing are set by the protocol and shown in the portal when you act; verify them there rather than trusting any blog, including this one.
